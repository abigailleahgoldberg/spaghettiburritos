# Celebrity Passover 2026: Who's Hosting, Who's Attending, and Who Definitely Forgot the Matzah

Passover starts Saturday night and you know what that means: celebrities posting extremely personal Seder content that they definitely workshopped with their publicist first. We're here for all of it. Every single bite.

## The Instagram Seder Industrial Complex

If there's one thing we've learned about Jewish Hollywood in the social media era, it's that the Passover Seder has become the ultimate soft-power cultural moment. It's family! It's tradition! It's an opportunity to post a photo that says "I have both a famous family and a genuinely beautiful dining room!"

The Spielbergs are hosting, obviously — this is not a rumor, this is just what the Spielbergs do. The table will be tasteful, the Haggadah will probably be a first edition of something, and the afikomen will be found by a child who has been preparing for this moment since December.

Barbra Streisand will post something about Passover that manages to be both genuinely moving about the liberation narrative and also somehow connected to her memoir. This is not a criticism. This is a talent.

- At least three celebrities will post about how much they love matzah ball soup with a level of genuine passion that suggests they should just eat matzah ball soup year-round and report back.

## The Haggadah Gap in Hollywood

Here's a piece of real Passover intel: there is a significant generational divide in Hollywood Seders between families who use an actual traditional Haggadah and families who use whatever version of the Maxwell House Haggadah survives in the house, and families who have "a Haggadah" that is actually a collection of printed papers, some laminated, from a ceremony done in 2007 that someone put in a folder.

The laminated papers faction is the most common and the most vigorously defended. You will not convince these people to get an actual Haggadah. They have everything they need. The folder is in the third drawer.

## What Actually Matters

Behind all the tabloid content — and we say this as people who bring you tabloid content — Passover is genuinely beautiful. The story of a people who were enslaved and became free. The obligation to tell the story as if you personally came out of Egypt. The table full of family, the specific foods that exist nowhere else, the songs that somehow everyone knows even though nobody has ever practiced them.

The celebrity Seders are probably a lot like your Seder: a little chaotic, a little rushed in some parts, someone forgot the horseradish at Ralphs, the youngest kid nailed the Four Questions, and somehow by the end everyone felt something real.

That's the whole thing. That's always been the whole thing.

Chag Sameach, Hollywood. We'll see the content on our feeds.
