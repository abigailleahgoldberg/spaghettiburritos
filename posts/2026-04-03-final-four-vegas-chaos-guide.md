# The Final Four Is Here and Vegas Is LIVING — Your Complete Weekend Chaos Guide

Oh, you thought Vegas needed another reason to go completely unhinged? Meet the 2025 Final Four weekend. The Strip is packed, the sportsbooks have run out of counter space, and somewhere right now a tourist from Indiana is telling a blackjack dealer about his team's bracket destiny. We love this city.

Here's your completely unnecessary but completely essential guide to surviving — and thriving in — Final Four madness in Vegas.

## The Sportsbook Situation

Let's start with the obvious: every sportsbook on the Strip is currently at approximately 200% capacity. MGM Grand? There is physically no room. Caesars Sportsbook? They added chairs in the parking structure. BetMGM's Aria location? A man reportedly slept there overnight Thursday to secure a prime viewing spot. Respect.

The move for anyone who is not a professional gambler with a comp package: Circa Sports in downtown Las Vegas. Yes, it's technically not on the Strip. Yes, it's worth the Uber. The stadium seating, the wall-to-wall screens, and the fact that they built the whole thing specifically for this kind of chaos means it handles Final Four weekend better than any property in town. The lines are still long. The experience is better.

Dark horse option: The Cosmopolitan's sportsbook is underrated and somehow still has people who don't know about it. The regulars are going to be very annoyed at us for writing this.

## Celebrity Sightings (The Verified, The Rumored, and The Entirely Made Up)

Look, it's Final Four weekend in Vegas in 2025. The celebs are here. We're not naming names because our lawyer has asked us to develop what she calls "a healthier relationship with the phrase allegedly." 

What we can tell you: there is a very famous former NFL quarterback at a certain high-roller lounge who has reportedly dropped enough money on the games this weekend to fund a small school district. There is a musician who should be on tour but apparently decided the Final Four was a better use of their Saturday. And at least three social media celebrities have filmed content in front of welcome signs and then gotten immediately lost looking for their hotel.

This is Vegas. Everybody is here. Keep your eyes open and your phone charged.

## The Party Circuit

**Thursday night (already legendary):** The pre-Final Four parties were, by all accounts, completely unreasonable. Hotel pools were at capacity. Clubs turned away hundreds. A DJ reportedly played a set that could be heard from three hotels over. Las Vegas doing what Las Vegas does.

**Friday:** The preparation day. This is when experienced Vegas visitors hydrate, sleep, and eat a meal that didn't come from a stadium counter. Rookie mistake: going as hard Friday as Saturday and showing up to the Saturday games looking like something the desert dragged in. Don't be a cautionary tale.

**Saturday (game day):** The championship energy is peak. Whatever you're doing on Saturday, you're probably doing it right if you can see a screen. The city organizes itself around the games in a way that is genuinely beautiful if you appreciate sports civic religion.

**Sunday (recovery/championship):** The people still standing on Sunday are the heroes of the weekend. They've earned their place.

## Food You Should Actually Eat

Between the gambling, the celebrity-watching, and the general chaos, you need to eat. Real food. Here are three places that will make you feel human again:

**Eggslut** (Cosmopolitan) for breakfast/brunch — yes it's a chain now but the Fairfax sandwich remains one of the more perfect morning foods in the known universe.

**Estiatorio Milos** for if you actually want a real meal and don't feel like mortgaging your house to eat at Joël Robuchon. Excellent fish, reasonable portions, people-watching that is legitimately excellent.

**Any Mexican restaurant on East Charleston** for when the Strip has broken you spiritually and you just want a $14 burrito that will restore your faith in humanity. The places that are not aimed at tourists are almost uniformly great.

## The Things Nobody Tells You

**The cab lines at 2 AM are a different sport entirely.** Get Lyft surge pricing or just... walk. The Strip is walkable if you accept that it will take longer than it looks on the map because every casino is the size of a zip code.

**The casino floors will be louder than usual.** This seems obvious but the specific combination of sports betting energy and regular gambling energy creates an acoustic environment that is genuinely remarkable. If you're noise-sensitive, the pool/outdoor areas are your friend.

**The hotels are not cheaper this weekend.** If you're just realizing this now, we're sorry for you. If you're staying anyway, welcome to the brotherhood of people who paid rack rate during a major event. There are more of us than you'd think.

**Hydration is load-bearing this weekend.** Vegas is a desert. The games are exciting. The drinks are everywhere. Drink water. We cannot stress this enough. Drink water.

Have a beautiful, chaotic, memorable Final Four weekend. Vegas will take care of the rest.
